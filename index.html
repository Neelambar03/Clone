About Nature

Nature is an inherent character or constitution, particularly of the ecosphere or the universe as a whole. In this general sense, nature refers to the laws, elements, and phenomena of the physical world, including life. Although humans are part of nature, human activity or humans as a whole are often described as at odds, or separate from nature.

During the rise of the modern scientific method, nature was seen as passive and governed by divine laws. With the Industrial Revolution, nature was redefinedâ€”either sacred, or a background to human development. Thinkers like Rousseau and Darwin revived a vitalist view of nature.

Today, nature refers to living beings, inanimate processes like weather and geology, and the wilderness untouched by human alteration. It reflects a contrast between what is natural and what is artificial.
